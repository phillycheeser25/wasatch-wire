Kyle Whittingham spent 21 years building something at Utah. Six conference championships, two Rose Bowl appearances, a program that made the jump from mid-major afterthought to legitimate Big 12 contender, and a culture so embedded in the fabric of Salt Lake City that it felt permanent. Then Michigan called, and he answered.

Morgan Scalley, Whittingham's defensive coordinator for the better part of a decade, was the obvious succession candidate and the right one. He was hired from within, which keeps some continuity intact, and he is a Utah man in the way that matters — not just professionally, but personally. He played there, he built his coaching career there, he understands what the program is and what it can become.

What he cannot control is the exit traffic that followed his mentor to Ann Arbor.

## What left with Whittingham

The most damaging departure was edge rusher John Henry Daley, a two-time All-Big 12 selection who was the anchor of a defense that had carried this program through multiple rough offensive stretches. Daley gave Whittingham's Michigan defense an immediate difference-maker. He left a Utah defense with a genuine vacancy at its most important position.

Beyond Daley, the offensive line underwent near-total turnover — not through defection but through graduation and attrition. The Utes must rebuild both tackles and at least one guard, which is the kind of structural challenge that turns 10-win seasons into 8-win seasons before the coaching staff has time to course-correct.

## Scalley's canvas

The fair counter-argument is that Scalley is not inheriting ruins. He is inheriting a program with real recruiting momentum, a quarterback room that features genuine options, and a receiving corps that is deeper than it has been in years. Kyri Shoels arrived through the portal with 59 receptions and 768 yards from San José State. Larry Simmons, Creed Whittemore, Tobias Merriweather, and Daidren Zipperer are all back. The offensive weapons are there if the line can give them time.

On defense, Scalley built the unit that is now the foundation of Michigan's staff. He has the credibility to recruit that side of the ball, and the scheme carries over even if the personnel don't.

## Schedule as savior

Utah's path to a double-digit win season runs through a schedule that was built for this moment. The Utes avoid Texas Tech. BYU, Houston, and Arkansas all come to Rice-Eccles Stadium. The road games are challenging but manageable, and there is no stretch where three Power programs line up in consecutive weeks.

If the offensive line gels — if the early cohesion work in fall camp translates to September Saturdays — Utah has the ceiling of a Big 12 title contender. If the line struggles, the Utes' defense will need to carry a heavier load than Scalley should have to ask of it in Year 1.

The 8.5 win total feels like a floor with a significant upside. In a new era, that might be exactly where you want to start.
