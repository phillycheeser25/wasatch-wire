There is a version of this preview that starts with a disclaimer — "despite losing their defensive coordinator" or "with questions at receiver" — the kind of hedging that keeps sports writers from looking foolish when October turns. That version of the preview would be dishonest. BYU is legitimately good, they know it, and the rest of the Big 12 knows it too.

The Cougars finished 12-2 last season, won the Pop-Tarts Bowl, and came within a tiebreaker of reaching the College Football Playoff. They return the most productive backfield in the conference, three veteran offensive linemen, and a defense that gave up just 19.1 points per game. Kalani Sitake turned down Penn State to stay. His offensive coordinator gave him a short-term contract extension because he can see what's coming.

What's coming is a BYU team built to compete for a national title, not just a conference championship.

## Bear Bachmeier, Year 2

The quarterback situation alone separates the Cougars from most of the top 15. Bachmeier arrived on campus in June of his freshman year, barely had time to learn the playbook, and went 65% for 3,033 yards and 15 touchdowns against seven interceptions in 14 games. For a true freshman who showed up late, that is a remarkable baseline. For a sophomore with a full offseason, it is a springboard.

The questions about Bachmeier have nothing to do with talent and everything to do with the sample size. When defenses have a full calendar year to prepare for him, can he adapt? Spring practice suggested he can. His footwork in the pocket was noticeably cleaner. His pre-snap reads came faster. The confidence of a player who knows he belongs looked different from the confidence of a player who is still figuring out if he belongs.

## LJ Martin and the run game

If Bachmeier is the headline, LJ Martin is the story that makes the headline possible. The senior running back ran for 1,305 yards last season, won Big 12 Offensive Player of the Year, and enters 2026 as one of the three or four best backs in the country. Every All-American projection has him in the mix.

What makes Martin genuinely dangerous is that BYU does not need to hide him. The Cougars can throw the ball, and opposing defenses know that. When a coordinator has to account for Bachmeier's arm and Martin's legs simultaneously, the run game becomes even more explosive than the raw number suggests. BYU averaged 5.4 yards per carry last season with Martin as the centerpiece.

## Defensive transition

The one legitimate concern is Jay Hill's departure. Hill built one of the conference's most efficient defenses over three seasons, and replacing that expertise is not a given. Kelly Poppinga takes over as coordinator — a former BYU linebacker who has been in the program's defensive room for years — and he inherits a unit with real continuity.

Ten of sixteen primary defenders return. Isaiah Glasker and Siale Esera form one of the better linebacker pairings in the conference. Cornerbacks Evan Johnson and Therrian Alexander III are legitimate lockdown players who will draw the opponent's best receivers. Safety Faletau Satuala at 6-foot-4 is a potential All-American.

The scheme may look different. The results should be similar.

## Schedule outlook

BYU's road through the Big 12 will run directly through Texas Tech and Utah. The Tech game will likely decide the conference's top seed. The Holy War will be the most watched game in the Mountain West all season, and BYU enters it as the favorite for the first time in years.

If Bachmeier takes the step everyone is projecting, if Martin wins All-American, and if Poppinga's defense holds its shape, this program is not a 12-win team. It's a 14-win team. That conversation is no longer premature.
