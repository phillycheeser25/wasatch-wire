They have been playing this game for over a century, and it still does not behave like other rivalries. The Holy War does not have a clean narrative arc. It does not reward the favorite. It does not care about rankings, win totals, or which program had the better recruiting class. It operates on a different logic — the kind of logic that produces 10-point underdogs leaving with the trophy and the fans who drove three hours to watch going home stunned.

That is not a cautionary note for BYU. It is a description of what both teams understand about this game when they line up on the same field.

## BYU arrives as the more complete team

This is not a sentence that would have been written five years ago, but the record demands it now. BYU went 12-2 in 2025 with a roster built around continuity and scheme. Bear Bachmeier is back as a sophomore who has already proven he can operate against top-half Big 12 defenses. LJ Martin runs behind three returning senior offensive linemen. The defense held opponents to under 20 points per game and returns ten of its sixteen key contributors.

Utah, meanwhile, is writing the first chapter of a new book. Morgan Scalley is a first-year head coach replacing one of the longest-tenured and most successful coaches in program history. Key contributors left for Ann Arbor. The offensive line is new. The question marks are real.

BYU is the more stable program heading into this season. That is just the current state of the rivalry.

## Utah does not need to be the more complete team

Ask any Utah fan what they think about being the underdog in the Holy War and they will tell you directly: good. The Utes have won this game as double-digit underdogs. They have won it when the BYU roster was clearly superior. They have won it in ways that defied explanation and did not require one, because the game has its own explanation built in.

Scalley inherits a team that is not short on edge. The skill positions are loaded. The defense, even without Daley, has talent and experience. A program playing its first game under a new head coach in two decades carries a certain kind of motivation that does not show up in win totals or Vegas lines.

## What actually matters

This game will not be decided by preseason rankings or conference projections. It will be decided by whoever controls the line of scrimmage, whoever makes fewer critical mistakes, and whoever in the secondary can cover the other side's weapons for sixty minutes.

That is the same answer as every other year. It is the only answer that has ever mattered in this game.

The rivalry record in this dynasty is 0-0. By the end of the season, someone will lead it. That someone will be discussed until next fall.
