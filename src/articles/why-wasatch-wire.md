There is a mountain range in Utah that most people outside the state have never thought about. It runs roughly 160 miles, north to south, forming the wall between the Wasatch Front's cities and the desert beyond. The Salt Lake Valley sits on its western slope. Provo sits just a little farther down the same slope. The mountains themselves do not care which city is which. They predate both universities by a few hundred million years and will outlast both programs by considerably longer.

The Wasatch Range is the reason we called this Wasatch Wire.

## Thirty-five miles and a world apart

Rice-Eccles Stadium in Salt Lake City and LaVell Edwards Stadium in Provo are 35 miles apart. You can drive between them in under an hour on a good day. The two schools share a media market, share a conference, share a state, and have spent the better part of a century convincing themselves they share nothing at all.

That's the rivalry. It doesn't need explaining to anyone who grew up in Utah. It does need context for anyone watching from outside the state, which is increasingly the case now that both programs are in the Big 12 and chasing playoff bids on national television.

The Wasatch Mountains sit between Salt Lake City and Provo like a referee who stopped talking to both sides a long time ago. The range doesn't lean red. It doesn't lean blue. It just sits there, enormous and indifferent, forming the backdrop of every stadium shot, every tailgate, every November game where something significant is on the line.

## A wire, not a wire service

Wasatch Wire is not trying to be neutral in the sense of having no opinions. It's trying to be neutral in the sense of covering both programs honestly — calling Utah's weaknesses weaknesses, calling BYU's strengths strengths, and refusing to let allegiance drive the coverage in either direction.

This dynasty features both schools in the Big 12 at the same time, competing in the same conference, with a Holy War game that will have playoff implications more years than not. That story deserves coverage that can hold both perspectives simultaneously without flinching.

The mountain does that. It holds both cities in its shadow without picking a side. We thought that was the right name for what we're trying to do here.

## What's coming

This site will cover every game, every week. Scores, recaps, stat breakdowns, standings, rankings — all of it updated as the season moves. The Holy War gets its own page because it deserves one. The Tabloids section covers the player drama and campus gossip that makes a dynasty feel like a living world rather than a video game scoreboard.

When the season starts, game screenshots go in the inbox, articles go up, and the Wasatch Wire becomes a live record of wherever this dynasty goes.

The mountains will still be there when it's over. The games are the part that disappears. That's why we write them down.
