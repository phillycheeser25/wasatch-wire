Indiana enters 2026 as something no program has been in years: a defending national champion with nearly everyone back. The Hoosiers went 16-0 a season ago, becoming just the second team in the playoff era to run the table, and they open as the clear consensus No. 1 in every preseason poll worth reading. It is a position earned rather than assumed, and it comes with a target the size of Memorial Stadium.

But this is college football, and coronations are for other sports. Seven of the last ten preseason No. 1 teams failed to win the national title. Somebody on this list is going to make Indiana earn it again.

## The national picture

Ohio State is the consensus No. 2, returning quarterback Julian Sayin, receiver Jeremiah Smith, and running back Bo Jackson — a trio that made the Buckeyes' offense the most feared in the country last fall and a legitimate threat to knock Indiana off in January. Miami and Notre Dame follow, both programs convinced they have finally assembled the roster to get to the final game. Oregon, Georgia, Penn State, and Tennessee round out the top eight, giving this preseason ballot more legitimate contenders at the top than any in recent memory.

The SEC put seven teams in the top 25. The Big Ten put six. Somebody in the Big 12 made them both uncomfortable.

## BYU belongs at 13

The Cougars arrive at No. 13 not because voters ran out of other ideas, but because the résumé demands it. BYU went 12-2 a season ago, won the Pop-Tarts Bowl, and returns the most dangerous backfield in the conference. Quarterback Bear Bachmeier — who didn't arrive on campus until June of his freshman year — finished with 3,033 yards and 15 touchdowns. Running back LJ Martin, the 2025 Big 12 Offensive Player of the Year, ran for 1,305 yards and has drawn All-American projections from every outlet that has bothered to look. Three senior offensive linemen are back. The defense loses coordinator Jay Hill to Michigan's staff, but Kelly Poppinga takes over a unit that returns ten of its sixteen primary contributors.

Coach Kalani Sitake turned down an overture from Penn State to stay in Provo. He is either very loyal or very convinced of what he sees in his locker room. Based on the roster, the conviction looks justified.

## Utah at 22: a new chapter

The Utes' ranking at No. 22 is the more complicated story. Kyle Whittingham, who built Utah into a legitimate power over two decades, left for Michigan. His defensive coordinator Morgan Scalley steps into the program's most consequential coaching transition in a generation. Several key contributors followed Whittingham north, including edge rusher John Henry Daley, and the offensive line underwent a near-total rebuild.

Utah's preseason win total sits at 8.5 — respectable for a program still viewed as a Big 12 contender, but a number that reflects genuine uncertainty about how quickly Scalley can put his own stamp on a roster that has a lot of new faces in critical spots. The schedule is favorable. The talent is there. The question is cohesion, and September will answer it fast.

## Big 12 has five

Beyond BYU at 13 and Utah at 22, the Big 12 placed Texas Tech at 17, Iowa State at 19, and Colorado at 20 in the preseason poll. Texas Tech was the conference's best team last fall by most measures and opens as the favorite to win the league again, though the quarterback situation changed when Brendan Sorsby entered the supplemental draft. Iowa State has quietly built one of the conference's most complete rosters. Colorado, riding the wave of Deion Sanders' recruiting momentum, finally has the depth to back up the hype.

The Big 12 does not have the SEC's depth or the Big Ten's brand ceiling, but it has something neither of those conferences can claim: the two most interesting rivalry games on any given fall Saturday. The Holy War and the Tech-BYU matchup will each draw national attention this fall, and at least one of them is going to have major playoff implications.

The preseason poll is a conversation starter, not a verdict. Ask the teams at the bottom of this list — they always seem to have something to say about it by October.
